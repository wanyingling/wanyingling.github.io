My research experience and interests centre on health inequalities and social determinants of health, and lie at the intersection of sociology, demography, epidemiology and social policy.

## Sociological Orientation: Work and Health

Using quantitative and qualitative methods to understand emotional work, self-identity and acculturation in cross-border labour markets, as well as the impact of employment status and job quality on the health and well-being of individuals

### **Relevant working papers and publications**:

- Working Time Mismatch and Employee Subjective Well-being Across Institutional Contexts: A Job Quality Perspective, with Senhu Wang (National University of Singapore) and Zhuofei Lu (University of Manchester).

- Work-Schedule Instability and Workers’ Health and Wellbeing in China, with Ya Guo (National University of Singapore), Wen Fan (Boston College) and  Senhu Wang (National University of Singapore).

- [Can Volunteering Buffer the Negative Impacts of Unemployment and Economic Inactivity on Mental Health? Longitudinal Evidence from the United Kingdom](https://doi.org/10.3390/ijerph19116809), **_Int. J. Environ. Res. Public Health_**, 2023, with Senhu Wang (National University of Singapore) and Zhuofei Lu (University of Manchester) et al.

## Epidemiological Orientation: Health Inequalities Under the Skin

Drawing on a series of biomarkers to identify health disparities "under the skin", and to reveal population health inequalities from a multidimensional perspective.

### **Relevant working papers and publications**:

- Measuring resilience using cortisol, with Tarani Chandola (The University of Hong Kong), Kumari Meena (University of Essex) and Stephanie Cahill (University of Manchester).


## Demographic Orientation: Healthy Life Expectancy

Developing methods and tools for estimating healthy life expectancy to elucidate trends in the expansion and compression of healthy life expectancy across countries and the sociological mechanisms of its inequality

### **Relevant working papers and publications**:

- Expansion or compression? Understanding inequalities in healthy life expectancy for the Chinese elderly from a life course perspective.

## Social Policy Orientation: Guardianship in Single and Ageing Societies

Drawing on the cutting-edge experience from the non-profit sector and industry in China to assess the needs for elder guardianship and the difficulties in practice, and to inform the development of refined policy and legal frameworks

### **Relevant working papers and publications**:

- Research Report on Guardianship of the Elderly(老年人监护问题报告). Sept 2023.

- [Marriage and Fertility Intention Survey of College Student (中国大学生婚育调查)](https://www.bilibili.com/video/BV1bu411v7Q9), with Ting Li (Renmin University of China), Yexin Zheng (Renmin University of China) and Yuteng Yan (Renmin University of China) et al,. March 2022.

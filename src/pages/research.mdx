My research experience and interests centre on health inequalities and social determinants of health, and lie at the intersection of demography, sociology and epidemiology.

## Demographic Orientation: Healthy Working Life Expectancy

Developing methods and tools for estimating and decomposing differences in healthy working life expectancy to elucidate the patterns of inequalities in healthy working life expectancy across populations

### **Relevant working papers and publications**:

- Expansion or compression? Understanding inequalities in healthy life expectancy for the Chinese elderly from a life course perspective.

## Sociological Orientation: Health Inequalities in Institutional Perspective

Using quantitative and qualitative methods, to investigate disparities in health inequalities and their mechanisms in different institutional contexts.

### **Relevant working papers and publications**:

- Working Time Mismatch and Employee Subjective Well-being Across Institutional Contexts: A Job Quality Perspective, with Senhu Wang (National University of Singapore) and Zhuofei Lu (University of Manchester).

- Urban-rural Differences in the Wealth Portfolio of Chinese People and Their Health Inequalities Across the Lifecourse, with Yang Zhang (Renmin University of China) and Jiaowei Gong (Renmin University of China).

## Epidemiological Orientation: Health Inequalities Under the Skin

Drawing on a series of biomarkers to identify health disparities "under the skin", and to reveal population health inequalities from a multidimensional perspective.

### **Relevant working papers and publications**:

- Measuring Resilience to Chronic Pain in Population Surveys Using Hair Cortisol, with Tarani Chandola (The University of Hong Kong), Stephanie Cahill (University of Manchester) and Kumari Meena (University of Essex).
